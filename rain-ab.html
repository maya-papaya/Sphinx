after the rain
by Allegra Bockhaus

My bare feet sang as I alighted on the wet grassy ground
Hours ago, The black storm clouds had burst
Like a vessel of ink in the sky
Tiny diamonds had rained down and popped on the concrete
And now the effects had been splattered far as I could see
And they could only lay in wait for the hot air to pick them up
And carry them through the air along
With the rusty robbins, jays, and the oiled and iridescent wings
Of tiny, mighty, insects
That bow to the inky pools
After the rain
