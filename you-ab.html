You
by Allegra Bockhaus

You are only to me as how, when, i have experienced you
Layers more of your soul sit within you and even more you have sprinkled
Like sugar
Into the world, the scar on your mothers stomach, the cashier who walks in with a frown but leaves with a smile

And some layers you have shown me, opening like a rose
The stories you have told me of the family that you chose
Some layers i have peeled back, like skin off bones
The conversations i've overheard while you sit with telephones

Some have come easy, crumbling from you like pastries
Your chipped tooth chipper smile chipping away at my heart
Some I will never know, will melt into you like silent film
Dissipating as they lay you in your grave
