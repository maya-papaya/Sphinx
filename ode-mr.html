Ode to the in-betweens
Maya Reddy

nothingness,
the liquid dark between stars.

what blinding light,
brilliance from which celestial objects are born,
do you hide?

are you a curtain,
an opaque veil,
shielding the corporeality of omniscience?

are you the skull,
our brevity the mere flickers sent down axons,
our existence an illusion?

are you the middle of two faces,
the divide between Janus,
where the skins fold into each other?

are you that uncertainty,
the “grey area”,
the reason for the impossibility of a universal law?
of unbendable virtue and goodness?

what master do you serve?
or are you the master
who forces all to their knees,
broken by the unsolvability of the unknown,

broken by you?
